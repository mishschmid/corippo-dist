Locarno Film Festival

Cinema Under the Stars at Europe's Most Beautiful Open-Air Theater

Every August since 1946, the lakeside town of Locarno transforms into one of Europe's premier destinations for cinema lovers. The Locarno Film Festival brings together filmmakers, critics, and enthusiasts from around the world for 11 days of screenings, premieres, and celebrations of the art of film.

At the heart of the festival is the magnificent Piazza Grande—Europe's largest open-air cinema. Each evening, 8,000 film lovers gather under the stars to watch international premieres on one of the world's largest screens, measuring an impressive 26 by 14 meters. The experience is truly magical, with the historic buildings of Locarno's old town creating a stunning backdrop to world-class cinema.

A Festival with History

Founded in 1946, the Locarno Film Festival is one of the oldest film festivals in the world, standing alongside prestigious events like Venice and Cannes. The inaugural evening took place on August 23, 1946, at the Grand Hotel of Locarno with just fifteen films—including classics like Roberto Rossellini's "Rome, Open City" and Billy Wilder's "Double Indemnity."

In 1971, the festival introduced its most iconic feature: the outdoor screenings at Piazza Grande. The massive screen has become such a symbol of Swiss culture that it's depicted on the country's 20-franc banknote. Today, the festival screens over 300 films across 13 venues, but the nightly Piazza Grande screenings remain the undisputed highlight.

The Piazza Grande Experience

There's nothing quite like watching a film under the stars in Piazza Grande. As daylight fades and the lights dim, the square transforms into an enormous outdoor cinema. The festival's signature yellow chairs fill the square, and the atmosphere buzzes with anticipation. The exceptional projection quality, combined with the warm August evenings and the architectural beauty surrounding you, creates an unforgettable cinematic experience.

The festival showcases a diverse range of films, from international premieres and competition entries to retrospectives celebrating legendary filmmakers. Whether you're a serious cinephile or simply looking for a unique cultural experience, there's something for everyone.

Why Visit from Corippo?

Staying in Corippo during the festival gives you the best of both worlds. After a day exploring the pristine nature of the Verzasca Valley, you can drive just 30 minutes to Locarno for an evening of world-class cinema. It's a perfect blend of mountain tranquility and cultural excitement.

The festival runs throughout the day, with screenings in various indoor venues alongside the evening Piazza Grande events. You could easily spend a full day in Locarno—exploring the lakefront promenade, enjoying lunch at a grotto, browsing the festival bookshops and exhibitions, and then settling in for the magical outdoor screening as night falls.

Practical Information

Dates: The festival typically runs for 11 days in early August (August 5-15 in 2026). Check the official website at locarnofestival.ch for exact dates and programming.

Getting there from Corippo: Drive to Locarno in approximately 30-35 minutes via the scenic Verzasca Valley road. During the festival, parking can be challenging—arrive early or use the PostBus service. Alternatively, drive to Tenero and take the train to Locarno station, which is a short walk from Piazza Grande.

Tickets: Individual screening tickets and day passes become available from April onwards. Festival Info Points open from August 4. Piazza Grande screenings are the most popular, so book in advance when possible. Day passes offer great value if you plan to see multiple films.

What to bring: Comfortable seating (cushions are helpful on the festival chairs), a light jacket for cooler evenings, and perhaps a picnic dinner to enjoy before the screening. The square has food and drink vendors, but lines can be long.

Best time to arrive: For Piazza Grande screenings, the square opens about two hours before showtime. Arriving early ensures better seats and lets you soak in the pre-screening atmosphere.
