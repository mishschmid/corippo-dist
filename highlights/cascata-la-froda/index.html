Cascata La Froda

Cascata La Froda - A Majestic Waterfall at the End of the Valley

At the very end of the Verzasca Valley, where the road gives way to mountain trails and the landscape feels almost untouched, you'll find one of Ticino's most beautiful natural wonders: Cascata La Froda. This stunning 70-meter waterfall plunges dramatically into a natural pool, creating a scene that feels like something from a fairy tale.

The hike to reach the waterfall is an easy 20-minute walk from the charming village of Sonogno, making it accessible for visitors of all ages and fitness levels. The path winds along the Redorta river, through lush forest, before revealing the thundering cascade in all its glory.

The Trail to the Waterfall

Starting from the car-free village of Sonogno, follow the signs toward Val Redorta. The well-maintained path leads you southwest past the historic church and along the riverbank. As you approach, you'll first glimpse the waterfall through the trees, and soon you'll reach a charming wooden bridge offering spectacular views of the cascade.

The Riale Carded river feeds the waterfall, and after heavy rainfall or during the spring snowmelt, the volume of water is truly impressive. On sunny days, you might even catch a rainbow forming in the mist at the base of the falls.

Explore Sonogno Village

Before or after your waterfall visit, take time to wander through Sonogno itself. This picturesque village at 918 meters elevation is the last settlement in the Verzasca Valley, and its traditional stone houses (rustici) with wooden balconies adorned with flowers are a photographer's dream.

Don't miss the Museum of the Verzasca Valley, housed in the historic Genardini building, which tells the story of the valley's past through about a thousand artifacts. Nearby, the Casa della Lana (House of Wool) showcases traditional wool-working crafts, where you can watch artisans transform sheep's wool into beautiful garments.

Practical Information

Getting there from Corippo: Drive north through the Verzasca Valley toward Sonogno (approximately 15 minutes). Alternatively, take PostBus 321 which runs regularly through the valley. Sonogno is car-free, so park in the paid lot at the village entrance.

Trail difficulty: Easy. The path is mostly flat and paved, suitable for all ages and mobility levels. Allow 20-25 minutes each way.

Best time to visit: Late spring and early summer offer the most impressive water flow due to snowmelt. After rainfall is also spectacular. Early morning visits help avoid crowds during peak tourist season.

Dining tip: Stop at Grotto Efra along the trail for traditional Ticinese cuisine with a view of the waterfall.
