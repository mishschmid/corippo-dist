üèä‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Swimming spots along the Verzasca

From Corippo Bivio up the valley, there are countless beautiful bathing spots. Probably the most impressive are between Corippo Bivio and Lavertezzo.

As a general rule, the left-hand side is only accessible on foot via the hiking trail, which also means that there are fewer bathers on this side. As the hiking trail runs through the forest, it is not always clear where the best paths down to the river are. A bit of trial and error is simply part of it.

The right-hand side can be reached by car and there are always a few parking spaces, but these are also quite popular. Most of the time it works, but you need a bit of luck - especially at the Ponte dei Salti.

Ponte dei Salti

The picturesque Ponte dei Salti is THE tourist hotspot in the Verzasca Valley and definitely worth a visit. It's also a great place to go swimming - but you'll never be alone there. A little further downstream, however, it thins out noticeably.

Dam, Corippo Bivio

Right at Corippo Bivio there is a first beautiful bathing spot. As the weir dams up the water, it is always very calm at this point, which makes it ideal for swimming - especially with small children. The weir is just a 5-minute walk from Corippo Bivio.

The old mill & the waterfall

The old mill and the waterfall behind it are just a stone's throw from Corippo and well worth a visit. From the main place where the church is follow the sign "Mergocia" for about 150 meters. There you see another sign "Mulino" - follow it you are almost there already.

The mill is still occasionally used today to grind chestnut flour - just like hundreds of years ago! The waterfall is also a great place to cool off üèä‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è
